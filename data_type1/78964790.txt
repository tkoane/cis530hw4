News And Scripps Networks Reportedly Lead Bidding Race For Travel Channel.
News Corp. and Scripps Networks Interactive Inc. are leading the race for the acquisition of Travel Channel Media with offers of about $800 million, four people close to the situation said. One person stated that Cox Communications Inc. is likely to cheese one party for exclusive negotiations by October 28. Bloomberg reported that the person denied to be identified as the talks are not public. The report stated that David Grabert, a spokesman of Cox, News Corps spokesman Jack Horner and Mark Kroeger, a spokesman of Scripps Networks denied commenting on the issue. The private-equity firms have been in talks with Discovery, which is available to run the channel should one of them win the auction, the people said. Shares of News Corp. declined by 46 cents and Scripps Networkss shares fell by 51 cents.
