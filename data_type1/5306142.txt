Parties Shows Interest In TGK-11.
A Russian court has frozen the sale of shares in TGK-11 as part of a second lawsuit filed by Neft Aktiv, a subsidiary of oil major Rosneft, a source close to the sale said. Its parent company, Unified Energy System, said it still hoped to go ahead with the sale of the firm and was pursuing legal means to do so. One of the firms interested includes Evonik Industries AG, which will not proceed with the acquisition until all of TGK-11's legal issues are resolved, sources close to the deal have said. AES of the United States, as well as Russian energy firms EvroSibEnergo and SUEK, have also shown interest in acquiring the company, whose secondary offering of 26% of its shares was to raise up to RUB 8 billion ($338.3 million).
